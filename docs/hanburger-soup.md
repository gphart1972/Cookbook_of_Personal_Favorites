# Hamburger Soup

A hearty, old-fashioned vegetable and hamburger soup that's easy to make and perfect for feeding a crowd.

## Ingredients

- 2 pounds ground chuck (80/20 preferred)
- 1 (15 oz) can diced tomatoes
- 1 can green beans, undrained
- 1 can green peas, undrained
- 1 can sliced potatoes, undrained
- 1 can sliced carrots, undrained
- 1½ cups chopped onion
- 1 cup chopped bell pepper
- 2 bunches green onions, chopped
- 1 cup diced celery
- 1 tablespoon chopped garlic

## Instructions

1. In a **5-quart or larger stock pot**, brown the ground chuck over medium-high heat until fully cooked.
2. Drain excess grease if desired.
3. Add the onion, bell pepper, celery, green onions, and garlic.
4. Cook for 5–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables begin to soften.
5. Add the diced tomatoes, green beans, green peas, sliced potatoes, and sliced carrots, including the liquid from the cans.
6. Add enough water to cover the ingredients by about 1–2 inches.
7. Stir well and bring to a gentle boil.
8. Reduce heat and simmer for **45 minutes to 1 hour**, stirring occasionally.
9. Taste and season with salt and black pepper as desired.
10. Serve hot with cornbread, crackers, or crusty bread.

## Notes

- The liquid from the canned vegetables adds flavor to the broth, so there is no need to drain them unless preferred.
- Ground chuck (80/20) provides the richest flavor, but leaner ground beef may be substituted.
- This soup reheats well and often tastes even better the next day.
- For a thicker soup, mash some of the potatoes into the broth before serving.

**Yield:** Approximately 8–10 servings
